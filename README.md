# 📚 Al-Ghazali Interactive Library

> مكتبة الغزالي التفاعلية — 52 interactive trilingual web apps based on the books of Sheikh Mohammed Al-Ghazali (1917-1996)

**Live site:** https://abourdim.github.io/al-ghazali-library/

## What is this?

A curated landing page for 52 interactive web applications, each based on a book by the Egyptian Islamic scholar and reformer **Sheikh Mohammed Al-Ghazali** (1917-1996). Each app features:

- Trilingual content (Arabic / English / French)
- Audio narrators via Web Speech API
- Interactive quizzes with XP, badges, and levels
- Multiple themes with day/night modes
- Age modes (Young Explorer / Teen Scholar)
- Offline-first PWAs

## Themes

The 52 books are organized into 7 thematic sections:

1. **The Noble Quran** (6 books) — Engaging with the Quran, thematic interpretation
2. **Sunnah & Hadith** (4 books) — Prophetic biography, hadith methodology
3. **Creed & Faith** (6 books) — Pillars of faith, spiritual reflection
4. **Ethics & Character** (8 books) — The Muslim's character and self-cultivation
5. **Society & Politics** (11 books) — Islam, economics, state, women's rights
6. **Dawah & Reform** (9 books) — Islamic preaching and contemporary issues
7. **Civilization & History** (7 books) — Cultural invasion, colonialism, Al-Ghazali's biography

Plus **Special Topics** (1 book) — Ramadan and fasting.

## About Sheikh Mohammed Al-Ghazali

Mohammed Al-Ghazali Al-Saqqa (محمد الغزالي السقا, 1917-1996) was an Egyptian Islamic scholar known as "أديب الدعوة" (The Orator of Islamic Call). He authored 94+ books across theology, ethics, and Islamic thought, defending a reformist, centrist interpretation of Islam.

## Disclaimer

These apps are **inspired by** Al-Ghazali's books — they are not verbatim quotations or replacements for reading the original texts. Content consists of simplified educational summaries guided by research about each book's actual arguments. For rigorous study, always refer to the original Arabic texts and qualified scholars.
